Permits & renovation rules
What needs approval, what can't be done, and when work is allowed.

Your ID handles the permit paperwork — but knowing the rules yourself helps you understand why certain things can't be done, and why timelines are what they are.

These cannot be done, by anyone
  • Hacking structural walls, columns, or beams — ever
  • Relocating the toilet bowl position (waste stack is fixed)
  • Extending wet areas beyond the original boundary without approval
  • Window works by a non-BCA-approved contractor
When work is allowed
Work typeAllowed hours
General renovation workMon–Sat, 9:00am – 6:00pm

This is also why "rushing" a renovation has limits — working hours are regulated, not negotiable. HDB also sets a completion timeframe for renovation works; your ID will confirm the current requirement for your block. Rules can change — verify current requirements on the HDB website or with your ID.

"When your ID says something can't be done — it's usually the rules, not reluctance. The waste stack, structural walls, and working hours are fixed for every ID in Singapore."

06 · During & After
What to expect
Know the difference between normal workmanship and a real defect.

The golden rule

Renovation is a craft — minor imperfections are part of it. Judge your renovation standing at normal distance under room lighting, not with a torch against the wall. The right question is: does it look right when you're living in it?

Normal — within workmanship tolerance
What you noticeThe reality
Grout appears patchy after drying Grout changes colour as it cures and dries at different rates. Judge after 48–72 hours. Persistent patchiness clearly visible at standing distance can be raised.
Wall feels bumpy or uneven to touch Plaster has a ±3mm over 2m tolerance. Smooth walls require skim-coat plastering — this must be specifically requested and is not included by default, especially in resale units.
Small gap between carpentry and ceiling Wood expands and contracts with humidity. Gap is sealed with silicon — the silicon line is the intended finish, not a shortcut. Slightly wavy silicon is normal; missing or pulling-away silicon is not.
Some wall tiles sound hollow Up to 20% hollow is the accepted industry standard for wall tiles. Floor tiles must be fully bonded — hollow floor tiles should always be raised.
Natural stone / timber veneer varies from sample Natural materials are unique — each piece has different grain, veining, and colour. No two are identical. View the actual slab before confirming installation.
Carpentry dimensions differ slightly from drawings Final measurements are taken on site. Minor differences are normal. Significant shortfalls from what was agreed should be raised at carpentry review stage — before finishing is applied.
Wardrobe doors slightly misaligned in first weeks Hinges and tracks settle with humidity. Request one adjustment visit — this is standard within warranty. Still misaligned after adjustment = raise as defect.
Aircon drips on first use Condensation in pipe on first run — normal and clears within minutes. Still dripping after 10 minutes of running — check the condensate drain.
This IS a genuine defect
