Plan Your Home First

Before speaking to any ID, spend time understanding how you actually live. Your answers shape every layout decision, storage requirement, and budget priority that follows.

Who is living here & how
People & future plans
  • How many people, and their ages
  • Couple only, with children, or with parents?
  • Plan for children in the next 3–5 years?
  • Will elderly parents join eventually? (Accessibility considerations)
  • Any pets, or plans for one?
  • Work from home? How many days per week?
Lifestyle & habits
  • Do you cook often, or mainly on weekends?
  • Do you entertain guests at home regularly?
  • Any hobbies needing dedicated space? (Music, art, fitness, gaming)
  • Current habits you want to carry over to the new home
  • How much storage do you actually need — count bags, shoes, clothes
Room by room — decide purpose before layout
RoomDecide this earlyWhy it affects design
Master bedroom Bed size, wardrobe placement, TV or no TV, dresser, study nook? Bed size sets the remaining wardrobe width. TV position needs an electrical point planned before works begin.
Bedroom 2 / 3 Guest room, study, nursery, shared kids room, or multi-purpose? Different uses need different electrical points, storage types, and furniture clearances.
Living room TV size and wall, sofa layout, dining table size, display storage? TV size determines sofa distance. Dining table size determines how much living space is left. Both affect carpentry drawings.
Kitchen Open or closed? Cooking frequency? Full appliance list? Heavy cooking needs stronger ventilation. Every appliance has a footprint that affects counter and cabinet layout.
Bathrooms Rain shower, handheld, or both? Bathtub? Instant or storage heater? Shower type affects plumbing layout. Bathtub needs structural assessment and extended waterproofing scope.
Utility / yard Washer only, or washer + dryer? Yard washing area? Additional storage? Dryer type determines if a wall vent is needed. All points must be planned before tiling the area.
Needs vs wants — separate them honestly
NEEDS — must have
  • ✓Enough storage for your actual belongings — count before planning
  • ✓Correct bed sizes for everyone sleeping here
  • ✓Kitchen that fits how you actually cook
  • ✓Adequate electrical points where you use devices daily
  • ✓Bathrooms that serve your household routine comfortably
NICE TO HAVE — be selective
  • ·Feature walls and display cabinets — beautiful but consume budget
  • ·Island / breakfast bar — measure available space first
  • ·Full false ceiling throughout — not all rooms benefit equally
  • ·Rain shower — check if water pressure in your block supports it
  • ·Large-format tiles — higher cost, more cut waste
3D drawings & virtual tour
What your ID should provide before works begin
  • Most IDs provide a 3D rendering or virtual reality walkthrough of your space before any work starts — use this to verify room proportions, furniture placement, and carpentry layouts
  • Walk through the 3D carefully: check bed sizes fit, wardrobe doors can open, kitchen layout flows logically
  • Changes are free at the 3D stage. Changes after fabrication or installation has begun are not.
  • If something looks off in the 3D — raise it. It is always easier and cheaper to adjust a drawing than a built piece

Project Timeline

A realistic picture of the entire journey — from the moment you start planning to settling in after handover. Renovation works typically run 8–12 weeks, excluding weekends, public holidays, and the first 15 days of Chinese New Year.

8–12 weeks before keys
Research & shortlisting
Set your budget, identify your needs vs wants, shortlist 3–5 IDs, go for initial consultations. Do not sign anything yet — this is a discovery phase.
4–6 weeks before keys
Select your ID, begin design
Sign your renovation contract, pay the 10% deposit. Begin space planning discussions, material selections, and 3D drawing review with your ID.
Key collection day
Site survey & documentation
Photograph everything. Your ID visits to do a final site assessment. BTO defects are reported to HDB. For resale, hacking scope is confirmed from site conditions.
Week 1–2 after keys
Finalise design & submit permits
Lock in all 3D drawings, carpentry layouts, material selections, and electrical/lighting plan. Your ID submits the HDB renovation permit (or MCST application for condo). HDB permit approval typically takes 3–7 working days.
Week 2–3
Hacking begins (40% payment due)
Demolition starts only after permit is approved. Floor tiles, wall finishes, old built-ins, and structures to be removed are hacked out. Visit the site post-hacking — this is when hidden conditions in resale units become visible.
Week 3–6
Masonry, plumbing & electrical works
New brickwork, waterproofing, screeding, and tiling are done. Plumbing and electrical works are carried out concurrently or in sequence depending on scope. Confirm all socket, switch, and light positions during this phase.
Week 5–7
False ceiling, painting & carpentry measurement
Ceiling boards go up. Walls and ceilings are painted. Simultaneously, carpentry is measured on site and sent for fabrication — this process takes 3–4 weeks from measurement to installation delivery. 45% payment is typically due at this stage.
Week 8–11
Carpentry installation
All built-in carpentry is delivered and installed. Countertops are fabricated and fitted. Glass works and door installations are completed.
Week 11–13
Final fixes & handover (5% payment due)
Lights, fans, accessories, silicon sealing, and touch-ups are completed. A joint walkthrough is conducted. Any items agreed to be attended to are listed and scheduled. Final payment is released upon sign-off, completing the project for both parties.
Post-handover
Warranty period begins
24-month workmanship warranty and 12-month waterproofing warranty take effect from handover. Any genuine defects identified within 7 days of handover should be reported in writing to your ID for rectification. Prompt reporting protects both you and your ID.

05

Renovation Phases

Each phase builds on the one before it. Understanding the sequence helps you know when to make decisions — and when it's too late to change them.

  • 01
    Hacking & Demolition
    Existing floor and wall finishes, old built-ins, and structures to be removed are hacked out. For resale units, this is when hidden conditions — extra tile layers, old piping, previous structural changes — are revealed. Your ID should review the site during or right after hacking.
  • 02
    Masonry
    New brickwork is laid where required. All hacked wet areas (bathrooms, kitchen, yard) are waterproofed — this is mandatory and cannot be skipped. Floor screeding is done to level the surface. Tiles are laid after the screed has set. Wall positions in wet areas are fixed by the original HDB layout and cannot be changed.
  • 03
    Plumbing
    Water supply and waste pipes are run to all wet areas. Whether this is done as concealed works (pipes hidden within walls and floors) or exposed works (pipes run on the surface) depends on your flat's existing structure, what works are feasible, and your design direction. Your ID will assess and advise — concealed works are not always possible and should not be assumed.
    Pre-2002 flats: original exposed pipes are common. Discuss with your ID whether to amend or retain.
  • 04
    Electrical Works
    Wiring, conduits, and DB box amendments are carried out. Whether works are concealed (within walls) or surface-run depends on feasibility assessed by your ID — not every flat allows for concealed electrical works throughout. Confirm all socket, switch, and light point positions before walls are closed up — relocating them after is additional cost.
  • 05
    False Ceiling & Dry Works
    Plasterboard ceilings, partition walls, and any dry-wall structures are constructed. All aircon trunking routes, lighting positions, and any cabling runs must be confirmed before ceiling boards go up. Note: false ceilings reduce room height — check your existing floor-to-ceiling height before committing to this scope.
  • 06
    Painting
    Painting is done before carpentry is installed, so walls are complete before built-ins go in. "Whole house" painting typically covers all bedrooms, living, dining, corridors, and store room — kitchen and balcony exterior walls are usually separate items. If you want smooth walls, discuss skimming or plastering with your ID before this phase — walls are not automatically skim-smooth, especially in resale units.
    Oil-based sealer is recommended for resale units — it prevents old stains, watermarks, and previous paint from bleeding through the new coat. This is a worthwhile addition, not an upsell.
  • 07
    Carpentry
    From on-site measurement to discussion, fabrication, delivery, and installation — carpentry typically takes 3–4 weeks depending on the amount of work. All drawings and material selections must be confirmed before fabrication begins. Changes requested after fabrication has started may not be feasible, and any changes that are possible will incur additional cost. Clear communication at the drawing and material confirmation stage is essential.
    Review your carpentry drawings carefully before sign-off. Confirm materials, dimensions, and internal configuration in person.
  • 08
    Final Fixes & Handover
    Lights, fans, accessories, silicon sealing, and touch-ups are completed. A joint walkthrough is conducted with your ID. Any items to be attended to are listed and an agreed completion timeline is set. The final 5% is released at sign-off — prompt sign-off after agreed items are resolved closes the project cleanly and professionally for both parties.

What to Expect

Renovation is a craft — most works involve human skill, site conditions, and natural materials. Going in with a clear and realistic picture of what is normal versus what genuinely needs attention helps the process go smoothly for everyone involved.

Before you read this section

The items below describe what is within normal workmanship tolerance in Singapore's renovation industry. Minor imperfections are part of the nature of the work. The standard to hold your renovation to is: does it look right and function properly when you're living in it?

If something genuinely concerns you, raise it in writing to your ID. A torch pressed against any wall, in any renovated home anywhere, will reveal imperfections. Judge your renovation standing at normal distance under room lighting — that is the fair and reasonable benchmark.

What is within normal workmanship range
What you noticeThe realityWhen to raise it
Grout appears patchy or uneven in shade after drying Grout changes colour as it cures and dries at different rates depending on humidity. Grout from different bags may also vary slightly in shade. Only after grout is fully cured (48–72 hours). If patchiness remains clearly visible at standing distance — raise it.
Wall feels slightly uneven or bumpy to the touch Standard plaster has a tolerance of ±3mm over 2 metres. Smooth walls require skim-coat plastering, which must be specifically requested and quoted — it is not included by default. If smooth walls matter to you, raise this before painting begins. After painting, skim-coat touch-ups are difficult to execute cleanly.
Small gap between the top of carpentry and the ceiling Plywood and board expand and contract with humidity. A small gap is expected and is sealed with silicon. The silicon line is the intended finish — it is not a workaround. Silicon that is pulling away, missing, or showing mould is a defect. A slightly wavy silicon line is not.
Some wall tiles sound hollow when tapped Up to 20% hollow area is the accepted industry standard for wall tiles. Hollow sound does not mean the tile will fail or come loose. Floor tiles must be fully bonded — hollow floor tiles can crack underfoot and should always be raised. Wall tiles beyond 20% hollow can also be flagged.
Marble, granite, or timber veneer looks different from the sample or showroom display Natural materials are unique — each slab or piece has its own grain, veining, and colour. No two pieces are identical, and they will differ from catalogue images and showroom samples. View your actual material slab or sample before confirming installation. Once installed, natural variation is not grounds for replacement.
Carpentry measurements differ slightly from the drawings Final measurements are taken on site, and minor dimensional differences from drawings are part of the process — site conditions are not always identical to what a drawing assumes. If a built-in is significantly off, or does not meet the wall or ceiling as agreed — raise this at the carpentry review stage, before finishing is applied.
Wardrobe doors slightly misaligned in the first few weeks Hinges and tracks adjust to humidity and door weight over the initial settling period. Request one adjustment visit — this is standard within the warranty period. If doors still don't close flush after adjustment, raise it as a defect.
Aircon drips water on the first use Condensation in the pipe on the first use is common — it clears within minutes once the unit is running. If dripping continues after 10 minutes of running — check that the condensate drain is clear.
What is a genuine defect

WaterproofingWater seeping to the ceiling below, adjacent walls, or outside the wet area boundary

ElectricalSockets that trip, flicker, or are not live at handover

When windows are changed — check the surrounding wall
  • Removing an old window frame often leaves the surrounding wall area uneven or with gaps where the old frame was seated
  • Plastering or making good the wall around the new window is recommended — confirm with your ID if this is included in your window scope or needs to be added
  • This should be done before painting so the wall is clean and complete behind the final coat

What Adds Cost

Most cost increases come from design upgrades — which is completely reasonable, as long as you know they're coming. Here's what typically adds to the initial quote.

What triggers itWhy the cost increasesRough impact
Upgrading bedroom or bathroom doors — from hollow core to solid laminated or veneer Rooms & Bathrooms Solid laminated doors for bedrooms and aluminium swing/slide doors for bathrooms are common upgrades from builder-grade hollow doors. Oversized doors above standard width (>860mm leaf) require custom fabrication. $300–$600 per door
Full height windows or windows with sill below 1,000mm PE Endorsement Full height windows (sill at floor level) and any window where the sill is below 1,000mm from the floor require a PE (Professional Engineer) endorsement for structural and safety compliance — this is a regulatory requirement, not an ID charge. $300 – $800 for PE endorsement
Upgrading kitchen cabinet doors from standard to glass Carpentry Tempered glass doors for kitchen upper cabinets or display units involve glass fabrication, aluminium framing, and more precise installation than standard laminate casement doors. +$300 – $600 per unit
False ceiling with decorative features Ceiling Works A flat false ceiling is the base cost. Each additional feature — cove lighting, layered bulkheads, curves, stepped levels — adds material and labour. Features compound quickly. +$800 – $3,000 per feature
Countertop material upgrade Kitchen Common options in order of cost: postform laminate (basic, heat-sensitive) → quartz (engineered stone, durable, most popular mid-range) → sintered stone (very hard, heat and UV resistant, premium) → natural stone (marble/granite, premium but requires maintenance). Each step up increases both material and fabrication cost. Quartz ~2× laminate; Sintered ~3–4× laminate
Feature wall Living / Bedroom Full-height feature walls in laminate, stone veneer, or wallpaper are large in area and require precise backing. Material and labour both contribute significantly. $1,500 – $5,000 depending on material
Tile selection above standard allowance Standard tile budgets cover a broad range. Imported tiles, large-format slabs, and textured or patterned tiles above the standard price point are charged at the price difference per psf. Varies — confirm psf rate with ID
Additional carpentry items Every additional piece — display cabinet, dry pantry, dressing table, study desk — is priced separately. Aluminium system wardrobes are priced differently from standard wood-based carpentry. $500 – $3,500 per unit
Concealed works (if exposed was originally quoted) Concealing plumbing or electrical works requires additional hacking, plastering, and materials. If your original quote assumed surface-run works, concealing is a variation order. Quoted as VO — confirm scope first
Design changes after fabrication begins Cut materials cannot be reused. Changes mid-fabrication mean new materials, new labour, and potential delay to the installation schedule. Avoid changes after drawings are signed off. Material cost may not be recoverable
Any item not in the original contract Anything added during renovation that was not in the signed contract is a Variation Order (VO). Always get a written VO quote and agree to it in writing before the work proceeds. Priced per VO item

Sanitary wares & basin — buying online (e.g. Taobao)
Key things to verify before purchasing overseas
  • Basin dimensions and mounting type: Measure your countertop cutout exactly. Undermount basins and topmount basins have different cutout sizes — confirm before ordering.
  • Tap hole spacing: Singapore plumbing fittings typically use 150–200mm centre-to-centre tap hole spacing. Overseas basins may differ — a mismatch means your tap won't fit.
  • Waste outlet (P-trap vs S-trap): Check which type your bathroom uses — not all waste fittings are interchangeable.
  • Electrical voltage: Singapore uses 230V/50Hz. Most Chinese appliances are also 220–240V, but always confirm for any electrically-powered sanitary items.
  • Shipping fragility: Ceramics are heavy and fragile. Confirm if the seller covers damage in transit — most don't.
  • No local warranty or after-sales support: If an item fails after installation, replacement and re-installation costs fall on you.
  • Contractor liability: Some contractors reserve the right not to warranty their own installation if owner-supplied materials are used and later fail. Clarify with your ID beforehand.
